Pipeline Stages

  • Data Cleaning — Replace '?' missing values, remove deceased patients, deduplicate by patient, drop high-missingness columns, encode features.
  • Feature Engineering — Age range → numeric, ICD-9 codes → 9 disease categories, medication summary features, ordinal lab result encoding.
  • Data Exploration — Readmission rates by age, diagnosis, race, insulin usage; correlation heatmap; time-in-hospital distributions.
  • Predictive Model — Gradient Boosting Classifier with balanced sample weights targeting early readmission (<30 days).

Dataset: Diabetes 130-US Hospitals

101,766 inpatient encounters from 130 US hospitals, 1999–2008.

Key columns:
encounter_id patient_nbr age gender race time_in_hospital num_medications A1Cresult insulin diag_1/2/3 readmitted
Target variable: readmitted has three values — NO, >30, and <30. We reformulate this as a binary problem: predicting whether a patient will be readmitted within 30 days (<30 = 1), which is the clinically significant outcome used by hospital readmission reduction programmes.

Why Early Readmission?

In the US, hospitals are penalised under the Hospital Readmissions Reduction Programme (HRRP) for excessive 30-day readmission rates. Identifying at-risk diabetic patients before discharge enables targeted interventions (medication review, follow-up appointments, patient education) that can prevent costly and potentially harmful early returns.

Early readmission (<30 days) affects {{ stats.readmission_rate }}% of diabetic encounters in this dataset — roughly 1 in 11 patients.
