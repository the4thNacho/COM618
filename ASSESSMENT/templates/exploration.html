Data Exploration

Patterns and trends in the cleaned Diabetes 130-US Hospitals dataset.

Readmission Class Distribution

The dataset is heavily imbalanced: only ~11% of encounters result in early readmission (<30 days). This imbalance is handled in the model via balanced sample weighting.

Numerical Feature Correlations

number_inpatient has the strongest positive correlation with early readmission — patients with more previous inpatient visits are significantly more likely to be readmitted early. Number of medications and diagnoses also show moderate positive correlation.

Readmission Rate by Age Group

Younger patients (under 30) and older patients (70+) show slightly elevated readmission rates. Middle-aged patients (40–60) form the largest encounter group but do not have disproportionately high readmission rates.

Readmission Rate by Primary Diagnosis

Injury and Circulatory disease patients have the highest early readmission rates, while Musculoskeletal and Digestive disease patients are least likely to be readmitted early. This information is captured by the ICD-9 category feature.

Readmission Rate by Insulin Usage

Patients whose insulin dose was reduced (Down) during admission have a notably higher early readmission rate — suggesting inadequate glycaemic management. The direction of dose change (not just whether it was prescribed) is a meaningful signal.

Time in Hospital by Readmission

Longer hospital stays are associated with higher early readmission probability, which may reflect greater underlying disease complexity. Shorter stays (1–2 days) also show elevated rates — possibly premature discharge.

Readmission Rate by Race

There is limited variation in readmission rates across racial groups in this dataset. Any differences observed should be interpreted cautiously given the imbalanced and potentially incomplete racial classification in administrative records.
