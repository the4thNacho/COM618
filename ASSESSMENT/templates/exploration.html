Patterns, trends, and cluster analysis on the cleaned Diabetes 130-US Hospitals dataset.

Readmission Class Distribution

The dataset is heavily imbalanced: only ~{{ readmission_rate }}% of encounters result in early readmission (<30 days). This imbalance is handled in the model via balanced sample weights (compute_sample_weight('balanced')).

Numerical Feature Correlations

number_inpatient has the strongest positive correlation with early readmission. Number of medications and diagnoses also show moderate positive correlation — consistent with the model's top feature importances.

Readmission Rate by Age Group

Younger patients (under 30) and older patients (70+) show slightly elevated readmission rates. The age midpoint encoding captures this non-linear relationship.

Readmission Rate by Primary Diagnosis

Injury and Circulatory disease patients have the highest early readmission rates, while Musculoskeletal and Digestive disease patients are least likely to be readmitted early.

Readmission Rate by Insulin Usage

Patients whose insulin dose was reduced (Down) during admission have a notably higher early readmission rate — the engineered insulin_down binary flag captures this directly.

Time in Hospital by Readmission

Longer hospital stays correlate with higher readmission probability, reflecting greater underlying complexity. Short stays (1–2 days) also show elevated rates — possibly indicating premature discharge.

Readmission Rate by Race

Limited variation across racial groups. Any differences should be interpreted cautiously given potentially incomplete racial classification in administrative records.
