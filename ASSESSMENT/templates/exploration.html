Data Exploration & Pattern Discovery

Mining the cleaned dataset for significant trends using Seaborn and Matplotlib visualisations.

Diagnosis Distribution

Insight: The dataset is relatively balanced between DIABETES (27 patients) and HEART DISEASE (25 patients), with 48 patients recorded as UNKNOWN (undiagnosed or missing diagnosis). The labelled subset is small but balanced enough for binary classification.

Feature Distributions by Diagnosis

Insight: Heart Disease patients tend to have higher Blood Pressure and Cholesterol levels compared to Diabetes patients. BMI is elevated in both groups. Age ranges overlap significantly, though Heart Disease patients skew slightly older.

Correlation Heatmap

Insight: Numerical features show weak-to-moderate correlations with each other, meaning they each contribute independent information to the predictive model. Blood Pressure and Cholesterol have the strongest positive correlation.

Smoker Status vs Diagnosis

Insight: Smokers have a higher proportion of Heart Disease cases, consistent with established medical evidence linking smoking to cardiovascular disease.

Gender vs Diagnosis

Insight: Both genders appear across both diagnoses. Male patients show a slightly higher rate of Heart Disease in this dataset.

Age vs BMI Scatter (by Diagnosis)

Insight: There is no clear linear separation between the two classes in the Age–BMI plane, which confirms that a non-linear model (K-Nearest Neighbors) is appropriate for this dataset. Both conditions span a wide range of ages and BMI values.
